Short Bio

I am a Wally Baer and Jeri Weiss Postdoctoral Scholar in Computing and Mathematical Sciences at Caltech, hosted by Leonard J. Schulman. I am broadly interested in theoretical computer science.

I received my PhD from UC Berkeley in the summer of 2019, where I was fortunate to be supervised by Professor Alistair Sinclair. My PhD thesis focuses on the interplay between phase transitions in statistical physics, locations of zeros of graph polynomials, and algorithmic questions such as the tractable boundaries of approximate counting, sampling and inference; an online copy is available here. Prior to that, I received my bachelor’s degree in the ACM Honor Class 2010 at Shanghai Jiao Tong University.
